Mental Health in the 21st Century

Mental Health in the 21st Century was a two week long intensive course run by Sarah Drummond, co-founder of Scottish service design firm Snook. The studio had a focus on developing service concepts that would improve mental health in Australia.

â€‹

I worked on finding ways that counselling services could be improved, using RMIT’s counselling service as a starting point. Through working with Jeremy Cass, head of RMIT counselling, my group and I developed a service concept that addresed issues identified by Jeremy, along with issues found through ethnographic research such as difficulties booking appointments, and keeping track of work set by counsellors. The service relied on a online hub for arranging appointments, communication and keeping on top of tasks and strategies developed through counselling, and linking up with pre-existing third party digital mental health services, such as MoodGym and Smiling Mind. The outcome of this studio was the development of a service that is easy to use and helps facilitate client engagement with the counselling service.
